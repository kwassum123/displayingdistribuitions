Visualizing Distributions

Distribution

Distribution is the term Cairo uses to refer to all of the values of a data set together; usually within the context of something visual or graphical. A distribution is visualized with a graph. He also describes what a normal distribution is and its properities. These properties being:

  1. Its mean, median, and mode are the same.
  2. The distribution is symmetrical: 50% of scores are above the mean, and 50% of scores are below it.
  3. We know what percentage of scores lay in between certain ranges: 68.2% of cases in the data are 1 standard deviation (sd) away from the mean, 95.4% are within 2 sd, and 99.8% are within 3.
  4. We can do some arithmetic with those figures.

The Elements of Distribution

A distribution has 4 elements that can be used to visually describe it.

Level
Within exploritory data analysis, the level of distribution is another term for the measures of central tendency, as they give you an idea of the average size of your numbers and what their centre point is.
Spread
The spread of a distribution tells how wide the set of data is. More specifically it could indicate the range of the data, the standard deviation, the variance, and the quartiles.
Shape
The shape of the graphical data indicates the dispresion/spread of the data, its variability, scatter, where the mean lies, and possibly what the range of the data set is.
Outliers
An outlier is any datum that lies on the outside of 1.5 times the Interquartile Range (the difference between the first and third quartiles) either over Q3 or below Q1. Any outliers are possibly due to variability within the nature of the data or may indicate experimental error.
